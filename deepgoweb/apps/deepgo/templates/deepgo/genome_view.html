{% elif not object.taxon and not object.infer_taxon %}

Inferred organism

not asserted for this proteome

Organism inference was not run for this example. Function-based taxon inference is reliable for eukaryotes (the eukaryote example resolves to Fungi), but not, at realistic genome scale, for bacteria, archaea or viruses: the predictor leans toward eukaryote-associated GO terms, and across a whole proteome those mispredicted only_in-eukaryote calls outnumber the organism's genuine taxon-specific signal (for this proteome, Bacteria is only a narrow runner-up to Eukaryota). Rather than assert a wrong organism, the example skips the call — the per-contig metrics, annotations and timing below are unaffected.

What do these mean?
coverage
Fraction of proteins that received at least one GO annotation.
completeness
Fraction of the domain's essential functions found on this contig (the right-hand column shows present / total essentials).
consistent
Whether the contig's annotations jointly satisfy the GO taxon constraints (SAT4J). FAIL means at least one violation.
violations
Number of taxon-constraint violations in the UNSAT core.
mean IC
Mean information content of the predicted terms — higher means more specific (rarer) functions.
composite
A single 0–1 roll-up of coverage, completeness, consistency and coherence.

Coherence columns (process / pathway / complex) are reported in the downloadable JSON; they show N/A unless the relevant evidence is present.
