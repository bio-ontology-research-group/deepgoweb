{# ---------------- SIDEBAR: how it works ---------------- #}

How it works

  • Genes → proteinsCDS from your GFF3 are translated (or genes are called if you omit it).
  • Function predictionEach protein is annotated with DeepGO-PlusPlus-Light against the Gene Ontology.
  • Infer the organismThe domain is read off the confident predictions via the GO taxon constraints (Asaad-style) — not assumed — and shown with its evidence.
  • Per-contig metricsCompleteness, coherence, consistency, information content — one row per contig.
  • Optional enforcementRemove taxon-impossible terms, impute essentials, repair coherence (SAT4J).
  • ProvenanceEvery assigned (or removed) function is traced to its source.

The examples

Four demo genomes, one per domain of life, each assembled by reverse-translating real proteins (so the predictions are genuine). The viral one is a complete genome — the whole phage T4 proteome (~290). The others are representative subsets of much larger proteomes: Bacteria (~450 E. coli proteins), Eukaryota (~150 baker's yeast) and Archaea (~150 M. jannaschii). The eukaryotic one infers the organism from the predicted functions — yeast all the way down to Fungi, not just the domain. The others run the full pipeline (per-contig metrics, enforcement, timing) but skip the taxon call: at genome scale the predictor leans toward eukaryote-associated GO terms, so for a bacterium, archaeon or virus the mispredicted eukaryote-only calls outnumber the true taxon-specific signal and the call is suppressed rather than assert a wrong organism. Predictions are reported down to a 0.5 confidence floor (the GO-DAG tail below that is pruned). No upload needed; each runs in a couple of minutes.
